# CerebroHive — Sales Plan

---

## 1. Sales Strategy Overview

CerebroHive operates across three revenue streams — consulting, automation, and education — each with different sales motions, deal cycles, and buyer personas. The sales strategy must account for this diversity while maintaining a unified go-to-market identity.

**Core strategic posture:** Land with consulting, expand with automation, retain with education.

The consulting engagement creates trust and organizational access. Automation products lock in operational value and generate recurring revenue. Academy programs maintain the relationship long-term and open doors to new buyer cohorts within the same organization.

---

## 2. Target Segments

### Primary: SMEs & Mid-Market (10–500 employees)

**Pain:** Manual, repetitive operations; no AI strategy; afraid of being left behind; cost pressure.

**Buyer:** Operations Director, CTO, or Founder/CEO depending on org size.

**Budget range:** $5,000–$50,000 per engagement (consulting + automation).

**Decision speed:** 2–6 weeks. Less committee, faster decisions, higher trust in relationship.

**Approach:** Direct outreach, LinkedIn, referrals. Lead with ROI and quick wins.

### Secondary: Enterprise (500+ employees)

**Pain:** Legacy systems, siloed AI initiatives, governance gaps, need for structured AI transformation.

**Buyer:** VP of Digital Transformation, Chief AI Officer, VP of Operations. Procurement is involved.

**Budget range:** $50,000–$500,000+ per year.

**Decision speed:** 3–9 months. Multiple stakeholders, RFP processes, pilot requirements.

**Approach:** Events, LinkedIn thought leadership, warm introductions, proposal-driven. Lead with strategic vision and risk mitigation.

### Tertiary: Students & Working Professionals

**Pain:** Career risk from AI disruption; lack of practical AI skills; no credible certification pathway.

**Buyer:** The individual (B2C) or their employer's L&D department (B2B).

**Budget range:** $200–$2,000 per individual; $5,000–$30,000 for corporate cohorts.

**Decision speed:** 1 day (individual) to 4–8 weeks (corporate).

**Approach:** Academy funnel — free content → email list → paid course or bootcamp.

---

## 3. Sales Team Structure (Startup Phase)

In the early stage, the founder carries sales with minimal support. Structure scales as revenue permits.

**Stage 1 (0–$500K ARR):** Founder-led sales. Every deal is a relationship deal.

**Stage 2 ($500K–$2M ARR):** Hire one Business Development Rep (BDR) for outbound prospecting and one Account Executive (AE) for closing.

**Stage 3 ($2M+ ARR):** Vertical specialization — dedicated AEs for Consulting/Enterprise vs. Academy/EdTech. Sales ops role to manage CRM and reporting.

## 4. Sales Process (Full Cycle)

### Stage 0 — Lead Generation

Sources ranked by priority:
1. LinkedIn organic content (inbound leads from thought leadership)
2. Referrals from past clients and network
3. Targeted outbound outreach (LinkedIn DMs, cold email)
4. Speaking engagements and webinars
5. Academy graduates and alumni (cross-sell into consulting)

**Qualification criteria (BANT):**
- **Budget:** Can they fund a $5K+ engagement?
- **Authority:** Are we talking to the decision-maker or an influencer?
- **Need:** Is AI adoption, automation, or upskilling an active priority?
- **Timeline:** Is there a decision expected within 90 days?

### Stage 1 — Discovery Call (30–45 min)

Goal: Understand the problem, qualify the deal, establish credibility.

Agenda:
1. Let them talk first — what's driving their interest in AI right now?
2. Probe for the real problem beneath the stated one.
3. Share one relevant case study or outcome.
4. Close the call with a clear next step (proposal, demo, pilot scoping).

**Hard stop:** If there's no clear problem, no budget signal, and no timeline — deprioritize. Do not chase.

### Stage 2 — Proposal / Demo

**Consulting:** Deliver a scoped proposal within 5 business days. Include: problem restatement, proposed approach, timeline, deliverables, investment, and why CerebroHive.

**Automation:** Live demo of relevant agent or workflow solution. Followed by a proposal with a pilot option (reduces risk, increases close rate).

**Academy:** Send curriculum overview, cohort dates, and pricing. For corporate, include a learning outcomes framework.

### Stage 3 — Negotiation & Close

- Primary lever: scope flexibility, not price reduction. Offer a reduced-scope pilot at a lower entry price rather than discounting the full engagement.
- Payment terms: 50% upfront, 50% on delivery for consulting. Monthly or quarterly for retainers. Full payment or installment for Academy.
- Contract: Simple services agreement for consulting. SaaS agreement for Automation products when available. Course enrollment agreement for Academy.

### Stage 4 — Onboarding & Delivery

Sales hands off to delivery with a structured brief. The sales rep stays involved for the first 30 days to ensure the client relationship is warm.

### Stage 5 — Expand & Renew

- At the 60-day mark of a consulting engagement, introduce the Automation division.
- At the 90-day mark, assess whether there are team upskilling needs (Academy cross-sell).
- Consulting clients who complete a project get a discounted retainer offer for ongoing AI advisory.

## 8. CRM & Sales Stack

**CRM:** Use a lightweight CRM from day one (HubSpot Free or Notion CRM as interim). Do not track deals in spreadsheets beyond 10 active opportunities.

**Pipeline stages in CRM:**
1. Lead (unqualified contact)
2. Qualified (BANT check passed)
3. Discovery (call completed)
4. Proposal Sent
5. Negotiation
6. Closed Won
7. Closed Lost

**Tooling:**
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator for prospecting
- Calendly or Cal.com for discovery call booking
- Notion or Google Docs for proposal templates
- DocuSign or similar for contracts
- Stripe for payment processing

## 9. Competitive Differentiation in Sales Conversations

When prospects compare CerebroHive to alternatives:

**vs. Big 4 / Large Consultancies:** We are faster, cheaper, and more specialized in AI. We don't have a generalist bench — everyone at CerebroHive lives in AI. You'll get senior attention, not junior associates.

**vs. Freelancers / Independent Consultants:** We offer a complete ecosystem — consulting, automation build, and ongoing education. A single freelancer can't cross-sell you into training your team or deploying an agent fleet.

**vs. AI SaaS tools (off-the-shelf):** We build custom AI systems fitted to your workflows, not generic tools you have to force-fit. And we train your team to use them.

**vs. Local/Regional players:** CerebroHive operates with enterprise-grade methodology at accessible price points. We bring the rigor of larger firms without the overhead.

# Pain Points Analysis

## Pharmaceuticals & Life Sciences -- Context

Pharma and life sciences companies using AI to accelerate drug discovery, optimize clinical trials, and automate pharmacovigilance.

## Overview

Detailed analysis of the most significant pain points in Pharmaceuticals & Life Sciences -- quantified where possible with industry data and CerebroHive client insight.

## Key Details

- Drug discovery cost ($2.6B average) and timeline (10+ years)
- Clinical trial recruitment and retention
- Adverse event signal detection speed
- Manufacturing quality variability
- Regulatory submission complexity
- Intellectual property protection
- Supply chain resilience
- Real-world evidence generation

## In-Depth Analysis

The pharmaceutical AI use cases generating the clearest business case in 2026 are: clinical trial patient recruitment optimization (reducing enrollment time by 25-40% through AI-assisted site selection and patient identification), adverse event signal detection in pharmacovigilance (improving safety signal detection speed by 50-70% through AI-assisted case processing), real-world evidence analysis (accelerating regulatory submissions by 30-50% through AI-assisted RWE dataset construction and analysis), medical affairs content generation (reducing medical communication production time by 60-70%), and commercial data analytics (improving sales force targeting effectiveness by 20-30% through AI-assisted HCP targeting and call planning).

CerebroHive's pharma AI validation framework is built around the FDA's Computer System Validation (CSV) requirements and the emerging AI/ML-specific guidance from FDA and EMA. Every AI system we build for pharma clients includes a validation master plan, installation qualification (IQ), operational qualification (OQ), and performance qualification (PQ) documentation, with ongoing performance monitoring that meets the requirements of the FDA's proposed AI/ML action plan for software as a medical device.

CerebroHive's Pharmaceutical & Life Sciences practice serves pharmaceutical companies, biotechs, contract research organizations, and medical device makers deploying AI across drug discovery, clinical trials, regulatory submission, pharmacovigilance, and commercial operations. Pharma AI operates under the most complex regulatory framework of any industry: FDA 21 CFR Part 11 for electronic records, GxP compliance for clinical and manufacturing AI systems, ICH E6(R2) requirements for AI-assisted clinical trial data management, and the FDA's emerging AI/ML-based software as a medical device (SaMD) guidance.

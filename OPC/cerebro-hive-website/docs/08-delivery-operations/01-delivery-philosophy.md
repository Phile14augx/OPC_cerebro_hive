# Delivery Philosophy

## Purpose & Scope

CerebroHive's delivery values: outcome obsession, quality, transparency, and client partnership in action.

## Standard & Requirements

CerebroHive's quality assurance framework applies at four points in every engagement: architecture review (before build begins, a Principal Architect reviews the technical design against CerebroHive standards and the specific requirements of the client's regulatory environment), code review (all code undergoes peer review against CerebroHive's coding standards and AI-specific quality requirements), pre-production review (a structured pre-launch checklist covering security, monitoring, documentation, and disaster recovery is completed before every production deployment), and post-launch review (a 30-day post-launch review assesses actual business metric performance against the projected outcomes agreed in the engagement kickoff).

CerebroHive's delivery risk management framework identifies seven categories of engagement risk: scope creep (managed through change control process and scope registry), data quality (managed through data quality assessment and pre-build remediation), stakeholder misalignment (managed through structured stakeholder engagement and escalation protocols), technical risk (managed through architecture review and proof-of-concept phases), resource risk (managed through bench strength and partner network for surge capacity), timeline risk (managed through buffer scheduling and dependency tracking), and client change management risk (managed through change management planning as a required deliverable of every engagement).

CerebroHive's AI Governance Framework is built on four pillars: Transparency (every AI system's purpose, scope, and decision logic must be documented and communicable to affected stakeholders), Accountability (every AI system must have a named human owner accountable for its performance and ethical conduct), Fairness (every AI system that affects human outcomes must be evaluated and monitored for demographic bias), and Safety (every AI system must be designed with appropriate safeguards against the failure modes specific to its risk level and deployment context).

The EU AI Act's risk-based classification system divides AI applications into Unacceptable Risk (prohibited), High Risk (heavily regulated), Limited Risk (transparency obligations), and Minimal Risk (no specific obligations). CerebroHive applies this classification framework to every AI system we design, regardless of the client's jurisdiction, as it represents the most comprehensive current standard for AI risk governance. High-risk systems (which include most AI used in employment decisions, credit scoring, healthcare, law enforcement, and critical infrastructure) receive additional governance requirements: conformity assessment documentation, human oversight design, data governance controls, and post-market monitoring protocols.

CerebroHive's AI ethics review process is mandatory for all client engagements and applies to the AI system design before implementation begins. The review evaluates: the potential for the AI system to cause harm (physical, financial, psychological, or reputational) to individuals or groups; the fairness of the system's outcomes across protected demographic groups; the transparency of the system's decision logic to affected individuals; the appropriateness of human oversight mechanisms; and the data governance controls protecting privacy and preventing unauthorized data use. Systems that fail the ethics review at any dimension are redesigned before implementation proceeds.

## Process

CerebroHive's delivery model is structured around three principles: outcome accountability (every engagement has defined success metrics owned by the CerebroHive engagement lead), embedded transparency (clients see work in progress weekly, not only at milestone gates), and knowledge transfer (clients are measurably more capable at the end of every engagement than they were at the start). These principles are operationalized through specific practices: weekly outcome metric review sessions, shared project management tooling accessible to client stakeholders, and a structured knowledge transfer program in the final phase of every engagement.

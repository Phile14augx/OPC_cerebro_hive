## Content

CerebroHive's delivery risk management framework identifies seven categories of engagement risk: scope creep (managed through change control process and scope registry), data quality (managed through data quality assessment and pre-build remediation), stakeholder misalignment (managed through structured stakeholder engagement and escalation protocols), technical risk (managed through architecture review and proof-of-concept phases), resource risk (managed through bench strength and partner network for surge capacity), timeline risk (managed through buffer scheduling and dependency tracking), and client change management risk (managed through change management planning as a required deliverable of every engagement).

CerebroHive's document template library is a curated collection of battle-tested frameworks, tools, and templates developed from direct engagement experience. Each template represents the distillation of multiple engagement learnings into a reusable format that reduces the time required to produce high-quality deliverables and increases the consistency of CerebroHive's outputs across different engagement teams. Templates are living documents: they are updated quarterly based on engagement feedback and evolving best practices.

Using CerebroHive templates correctly requires understanding their structure: each template has a purpose statement (what the document achieves), a population guide (how to fill in each section), quality standards (what a good vs. mediocre version looks like), and an example (a partially completed version showing the level of detail expected). New CerebroHive practitioners are required to produce three template-based deliverables under Principal review before they are permitted to deliver client-facing documents independently.

CerebroHive's templates are organized by engagement phase (Strategy, Build, Operate) and document type (executive communication, technical specification, governance documentation, operational procedures). The full template library contains 25 templates covering the most common document types produced in AI consulting engagements. Each template is available in Microsoft Word format for client-deliverable documents and as a Notion or Confluence template for internal use.

CerebroHive's delivery model is structured around three principles: outcome accountability (every engagement has defined success metrics owned by the CerebroHive engagement lead), embedded transparency (clients see work in progress weekly, not only at milestone gates), and knowledge transfer (clients are measurably more capable at the end of every engagement than they were at the start). These principles are operationalized through specific practices: weekly outcome metric review sessions, shared project management tooling accessible to client stakeholders, and a structured knowledge transfer program in the final phase of every engagement.

CerebroHive's quality assurance framework applies at four points in every engagement: architecture review (before build begins, a Principal Architect reviews the technical design against CerebroHive standards and the specific requirements of the client's regulatory environment), code review (all code undergoes peer review against CerebroHive's coding standards and AI-specific quality requirements), pre-production review (a structured pre-launch checklist covering security, monitoring, documentation, and disaster recovery is completed before every production deployment), and post-launch review (a 30-day post-launch review assesses actual business metric performance against the projected outcomes agreed in the engagement kickoff).

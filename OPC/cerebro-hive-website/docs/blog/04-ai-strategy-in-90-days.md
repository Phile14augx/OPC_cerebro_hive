# How to Build an AI Strategy in 90 Days

**Category:** Consulting | **Date:** June 5, 2026 | **Read time:** 9 min read

> Most AI strategy projects stall because they try to solve everything at once. This guide outlines a 90-day sprint framework: assess, prioritise, pilot, and measure — with real deliverables at each stage.

---

## Why Most AI Strategies Fail

The most common AI strategy failure mode is not technical — it is organisational. Organisations spend months in workshops building comprehensive AI strategies that cover every business function, evaluate every technology, and produce a 100-page strategy document that sits on a SharePoint server and collects dust.

The 90-day AI strategy sprint solves this by forcing scope discipline: in 90 days, you will assess your current AI readiness, identify your top 5 use cases, build the business case for the top 2, and have the first pilot in flight. That is a strategy and an execution motion, not just a document.

## The 90-Day Structure

### Days 1-30: Assess

**Week 1-2: AI Readiness Diagnostic**

Evaluate your organisation's current AI capability across five dimensions:

1. **Data Infrastructure** — Do you have the data required for your target AI use cases? Is it clean, accessible, and governed? Most organisations discover that 40-60% of their highest-value AI use cases are blocked by data quality or access issues.

2. **Technology Stack** — Does your current cloud infrastructure, data platform, and software architecture support AI workloads? Key questions: Are you on a modern cloud platform? Do you have a data warehouse or lakehouse? Can your application layer consume AI-generated outputs?

3. **Talent** — Do you have the internal capability to build, deploy, and operate AI systems? If not, what is the gap, and how will you close it (hire, train, partner)?

4. **Governance** — Do you have AI ethics policies, model risk management processes, and the governance structures required for your regulatory environment? Missing governance is a deployment blocker for regulated industries.

5. **Executive Alignment** — Is there a funded, named executive sponsor for the AI program? Without this, no AI strategy survives contact with the quarterly budget process.

**Week 3-4: Use Case Discovery**

Run structured use case discovery sessions with each business unit: operations, finance, HR, marketing, sales, customer service, and product. The goal is to surface 20-50 candidate AI use cases — not to evaluate them (that comes next), but to document what business problems exist and what AI might solve them.

Useful framing questions for use case discovery:
- What decisions do you make repeatedly that take significant time?
- What reports do you produce that are mostly mechanical assembly of data?
- What questions do your customers and employees ask repeatedly that could be answered from existing information?
- Where do errors in manual processes create downstream cost or risk?
- What would you do if you had 20% more capacity in your team?

**Deliverable at Day 30:** AI Readiness Assessment report (scored maturity across 5 dimensions, gap analysis, top 20-50 use case candidates documented)

### Days 31-60: Prioritise and Design

**Week 5-6: Use Case Prioritisation**

Apply the AI Value Canvas™ framework to each candidate use case. For each, document:
- Business value estimate (quantified where possible)
- Data requirements and current data readiness
- Technical complexity
- Time to first measurable value
- Regulatory and risk considerations
- Dependencies and prerequisites

Populate the HiveMatrix™ priority framework: plot each use case on business impact vs. implementation feasibility, identifying Quick Wins (high feasibility, meaningful impact — start immediately), Strategic Bets (high impact, requires enabler investment), and Backlog (deprioritise).

**Week 7-8: Business Case Development**

Build detailed business cases for the top 2-3 Quick Wins. Each business case should include:
- Baseline metric (current state)
- Target metric (AI-enabled state)
- Implementation cost (technology, consulting, internal resource time)
- Annual value (revenue, cost, or risk)
- Payback period
- Key assumptions and risks

**Deliverable at Day 60:** Use case priority matrix, business cases for top 2-3 initiatives, go/no-go recommendation for pilot

### Days 61-90: Pilot

**Week 9-10: Pilot Design**

Design the pilot for the top priority use case. Key design decisions:
- **Scope:** Which specific workflow within the use case will the pilot cover? Narrower is better for a 30-day pilot.
- **Data:** What data is required, is it accessible, and what data preparation is needed before the pilot can run?
- **Success metric:** What single metric will determine if the pilot succeeded? Define this before the pilot starts, not after.
- **Human integration:** How will the pilot output integrate with existing human workflows? Who reviews the AI output? How are exceptions handled?
- **Technology:** Which LLM, which framework, which integration approach?

**Week 11-12: Build and Launch Pilot**

Deliver a working pilot — not a demo, not a prototype, but a system that runs in a real business workflow with real data and produces real outputs that a human evaluates. The pilot should be designed to generate the data needed to decide whether to scale.

**Deliverable at Day 90:** Working pilot in production, pilot performance data against success metric, scale/no-scale recommendation with supporting data

## Common Pitfalls

**Pitfall 1: Boiling the ocean**
Trying to cover every business function in the strategy and every technology option. Fix: Scope to your top 3 use cases for the pilot phase. You can expand later.

**Pitfall 2: No named executive sponsor**
Without a budget owner who is personally committed to the AI program, it will be deprioritised the moment a competing initiative appears. Fix: Identify and formally enlist the executive sponsor before day 1.

**Pitfall 3: Data preparation underestimated**
Data is always worse than expected. Fix: Do the data audit in Week 3 and let the findings shape use case prioritisation — not the other way around.

**Pitfall 4: Success metric ambiguity**
If you cannot define what success looks like before the pilot, you will not be able to evaluate it objectively after. Fix: Define the primary success metric in Week 9 and lock it before build begins.

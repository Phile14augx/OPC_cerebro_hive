## Framework Phases -- Detail

HiveMatrix™ evaluations incorporate 12 data inputs per use case: business value estimate (top-down executive input and bottom-up process analysis), data readiness score (quality, availability, and governance of required data), technology readiness score (existence of proven technical approaches), organizational change impact (people, process, and culture changes required), regulatory risk rating (EU AI Act classification, sector regulation, and data privacy implications), competitive timing (first-mover value vs. follower risk), integration complexity (connections to existing enterprise systems), talent availability (internal vs. external capability required), time to first value (weeks to measurable outcome), and three-year total cost of ownership.

The power of HiveMatrix™ lies not in the scoring methodology itself but in the structured executive alignment process it drives. Most enterprise AI programs fail not because of technical limitations but because of organizational misalignment on priorities. HiveMatrix™ forces that alignment early: the framework requires sign-off from the CEO, CFO, CDO, and CTO on the business value estimates and feasibility ratings before the portfolio is finalized. This alignment is documented and becomes the foundation of the AI program's governance structure.

HiveMatrix™ is CerebroHive's AI use case prioritization and portfolio management framework -- the systematic methodology for cutting through 50 potential AI use cases to identify the 5 that will transform a business. HiveMatrix™ evaluates use cases on a two-dimensional matrix: Business Impact (expected value creation in revenue, cost, or risk terms over 3 years) against Implementation Feasibility (data availability, technical complexity, organizational readiness, and regulatory risk). The output is a portfolio allocation recommendation across four quadrants: Quick Wins (high feasibility, medium impact -- start immediately), Strategic Bets (high impact, lower feasibility -- invest in enablers), Foundation Builders (improve feasibility for high-impact use cases -- fund as prerequisites), and Backlog (deprioritize for now).

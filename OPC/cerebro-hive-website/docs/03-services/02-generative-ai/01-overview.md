# Generative AI & LLM Engineering -- Overview

## Overview

Enterprise LLM engineering from system design through production deployment and ongoing operations.

## Scope & Approach

Enterprise generative AI deployment in 2026 faces a specific set of challenges that differ fundamentally from consumer AI applications: regulatory compliance (EU AI Act, GDPR, sector-specific regulations), data privacy (enterprise data must not leak into public model training), reliability requirements (production systems need 99.9%+ uptime with deterministic behavior), integration complexity (LLM outputs must connect to existing ERP, CRM, and operational systems), and cost management (inference costs at scale require careful architecture decisions).

CerebroHive's generative AI engagements are scoped around specific business outcomes: a contract review automation system that reduces legal review time by 70%, a customer service AI that handles 65% of tier-1 support queries without human intervention, a financial report generation system that reduces analyst time per report from 4 hours to 20 minutes. We do not build AI demos -- we build production systems with defined SLAs, monitoring dashboards, and governance controls.

CerebroHive's Generative AI Engineering practice helps enterprise clients move from generative AI experimentation to production deployment -- building the LLM applications, RAG pipelines, agent systems, and model fine-tuning infrastructure that deliver measurable business value at scale. We have deep expertise across the full generative AI stack: foundation model selection and evaluation, prompt engineering and optimization, retrieval-augmented generation architecture, fine-tuning and RLHF, inference optimization, and production monitoring.

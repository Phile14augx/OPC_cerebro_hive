# M&A AI Due Diligence

## Overview

A specialized AI capability assessment for M&A scenarios -- evaluating a target company's AI assets, technical debt, data infrastructure, model quality, and AI talent as part of acquisition due diligence. Produces a structured AI risk and opportunity report.

## Key Elements

- AI asset inventory and valuation
- Technical debt assessment
- Data quality and governance review
- Model documentation and reproducibility audit
- Key person dependency mapping
- AI risk report
- Integration complexity estimate

## Details

The Govern phase establishes the AI governance framework that will manage risk as the program scales. This includes: AI ethics principles and red-line policies, risk classification methodology aligned to EU AI Act risk tiers, model review and approval workflows, data usage policies, and the escalation framework for high-risk AI decisions. CerebroHive's AI Governance Framework (part of the CerebroSphere™ delivery system) provides a pre-built template that we customize to each client's regulatory environment and risk appetite.

CerebroHive's AI Strategy & Advisory practice is the entry point for most enterprise AI transformations. We bring a structured, evidence-based approach to the most consequential AI decisions executives face: which use cases to fund, which to deprioritize, what the realistic ROI looks like, how to sequence the build, and what organizational changes are required to sustain an AI program beyond the initial project. Our advisory work is grounded in our proprietary HiveScore™ AI Readiness Assessment, which benchmarks an organization against 87 dimensions of AI maturity across data, talent, infrastructure, governance, and culture.

Every AI Strategy engagement at CerebroHive produces three outputs: a prioritized use case portfolio (ranked by business value and implementation feasibility using the AI Value Canvas™), a funded implementation roadmap with quarterly milestones and defined success metrics, and an AI governance framework that ensures the organization can manage AI risk as capability scales. These outputs are not slide decks -- they are working documents that the client's internal AI team uses to run their program.

The practice is led by Principal Advisors with backgrounds spanning Fortune 500 AI leadership, management consulting at tier-one firms, and hands-on AI engineering. This combination of strategic and technical depth means our advisory engagements are grounded in implementation reality from day one -- we have built the systems we are advising clients to build, and we know exactly where the risks lie.

CerebroHive's AI Strategy clients typically engage in one of three entry points: an AI Readiness Assessment ($25K-$50K, 4-6 weeks) that produces a diagnostic and prioritized roadmap; an Executive AI Workshop ($10K-$25K, 2 days) that aligns the leadership team on AI strategy and surfaces the most critical decisions; or a full AI Strategy Development engagement ($75K-$150K, 8-12 weeks) that delivers the complete strategy, business case, governance framework, and implementation roadmap.

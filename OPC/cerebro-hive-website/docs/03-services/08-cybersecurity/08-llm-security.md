# LLM Security Controls

## Overview

Implementation of prompt injection defenses, output filtering, PII detection, and audit logging for LLM systems.

## Scope & Approach

CerebroHive's Cybersecurity & AI Risk practice addresses the security, privacy, and compliance dimensions of enterprise AI deployment -- an area that most AI consulting firms treat as an afterthought and that most cybersecurity firms lack the AI depth to address properly. We combine AI engineering expertise with security architecture and compliance knowledge to ensure that the AI systems we build and advise on are secure by design, not security-reviewed after the fact.

AI systems introduce a fundamentally different threat surface than traditional software: adversarial prompt injection (attacks embedded in user inputs or retrieved documents), model inversion (extracting training data from model weights), data poisoning (corrupting training data to manipulate model behavior), supply chain attacks on model checkpoints and third-party APIs, and inference endpoint vulnerabilities. CerebroHive's AI security practice addresses all five threat categories as part of every production AI deployment.

CerebroHive's cybersecurity services for AI programs include: AI Security Assessment (evaluating existing AI systems against the OWASP LLM Top 10 and MITRE ATLAS framework), Secure AI Architecture Design (embedding security controls into the design of new AI systems), Red Team AI Testing (adversarial testing of production AI systems), AI Compliance Assessment (evaluating AI systems against EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, and sector-specific regulations), and AI Security Operations (ongoing monitoring and incident response for production AI systems).

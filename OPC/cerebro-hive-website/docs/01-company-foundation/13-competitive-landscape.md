# Competitive Landscape

CerebroHive competes in a fragmented market across three tiers. Understanding each tier is essential for positioning, messaging, and handling competitive objections in sales conversations.

## Tier 1: Global Strategy + Technology Consultancies

These firms have massive brand equity, Fortune 500 relationships, and unlimited capacity -- but they are slow, expensive, and rarely provide senior engineering talent on AI projects.

| Firm | AI Brand | Key Weakness | CerebroHive Counter |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Accenture | Applied Intelligence | Generic, expensive, offshore delivery | Senior-led, specialized, outcome-tied |
| Deloitte | AI Institute | Advisory without engineering depth | We build what we recommend |
| McKinsey / QuantumBlack | QuantumBlack | Brilliant strategy, limited build capability | We go from strategy to production |
| IBM Consulting | IBM AI | Legacy reputation, complex sales | Agile, modern stack, faster delivery |
| BCG X | BCG X | Innovation lab positioning, small capacity | We scale where they prototype |

## Tier 2: Mid-Market AI Consultancies (Direct Competitors)

These are the most relevant competitors -- firms of similar size targeting similar clients. They vary significantly in quality, specialization, and delivery approach.

The competitive landscape for enterprise AI consulting is fragmented between large system integrators (Accenture, Deloitte, IBM), hyperscaler professional services arms (AWS, Azure, Google), pure-play AI boutiques, and traditional management consultancies expanding into AI. CerebroHive's differentiation lies in the combination of engineering depth that boutiques offer, the broad service portfolio that large SIs offer, and proprietary IP that none of them possess at comparable depth.

The global enterprise AI consulting market is estimated at $45B in 2026 and growing at 32% CAGR, driven by accelerating enterprise AI adoption following the generative AI inflection point of 2023-2024. CerebroHive is positioned to capture a disproportionate share of this growth by targeting the segment most underserved by current market participants: mid-market enterprises ($100M-$2B revenue) that need full-stack AI capability but cannot access it from the large system integrators (minimum engagement sizes are too large) or from boutique specialists (insufficient breadth).

Enterprise AI adoption is moving from pilot to production at scale. In 2026, 74% of Fortune 500 companies report having AI initiatives underway, but only 19% report AI deployed in core business processes at scale. This gap between initiative count and production deployment is CerebroHive's primary opportunity -- the firm specializes precisely in closing that gap through engineering excellence and operational rigor.

## Competitive Differentiation Summary

CerebroHive wins on three dimensions competitors cannot easily replicate:

1. **Proprietary IP** -- 10 frameworks + Quantiva ERP are assets that accelerate delivery and signal capability
2. **Outcome accountability** -- Fixed-price and outcome-based models that most competitors avoid
3. **Engineering depth** -- Senior AI engineers who can do strategy AND build in production

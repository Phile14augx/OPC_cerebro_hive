# Year 2 Financial Model -- 2027

Year 2 is about scaling implementation revenue, launching managed services, and closing the first Quantiva enterprise licenses. The team doubles in size and the first specialist hires join.

## Year 2 Revenue Targets

| Stream | Q1 2027 | Q2 2027 | Q3 2027 | Q4 2027 | Full Year |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Advisory | $200K | $250K | $250K | $300K | $1,000K |
| Implementation | $400K | $500K | $600K | $700K | $2,200K |
| Managed Ops (CortexOps) | $100K | $200K | $300K | $400K | $1,000K |
| Quantiva Licensing | $0 | $100K | $200K | $300K | $600K |
| Training | $50K | $75K | $100K | $125K | $350K |
| **Total** | **$750K** | **$1,125K** | **$1,450K** | **$1,825K** | **$5,150K** |

The firm's financial model targets 55% gross margin on consulting services, driven by a blended delivery model that uses senior architects and AI engineers for design and critical path work, and associates or managed offshore capacity for implementation tasks. Quantiva ERP targets 70%+ SaaS gross margin at scale. Training programs run at 60% margin once content is developed.

CerebroHive's go-to-market model targets enterprise accounts with annual revenues between $100M and $10B, with a primary focus on North America and secondary markets in the UK, Australia, and the Middle East. We enter accounts through C-suite relationships (CDO, CTO, CIO), typically with a diagnostic engagement (AI Readiness Assessment or Executive Workshop) that surfaces the prioritized use case pipeline and funds the strategy engagement that follows.

CerebroHive operates a three-revenue-stream model: consulting services (project-based and retainer), the Quantiva ERP product (SaaS licensing), and training & certification programs. This model is intentionally designed to generate revenue at three different time horizons -- consulting revenue closes in weeks, Quantiva licenses build monthly recurring revenue, and training programs create market awareness and talent pipeline simultaneously.

Consulting pricing follows four commercial structures depending on client preference and engagement type: fixed-price projects with defined scope and outcomes (most common for assessments and builds), time-and-materials retainers for ongoing operations and optimization work, value-based pricing for engagements where the outcome can be quantified in advance, and hybrid structures that combine a fixed delivery component with a performance-based success fee.

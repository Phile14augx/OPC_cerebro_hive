# CerebroHive — Marketing Plan

---

## 1. Marketing Strategy Overview

CerebroHive's marketing has one primary objective in Year 1: **build credibility and generate inbound demand** across all three divisions without a large paid media budget. This is achieved through consistent thought leadership, organic content, and community building — turning the founder's expertise into a visible, trusted brand asset.

The shift in Year 2–3 is toward **scaling what works**: amplifying high-performing content with paid distribution, launching the Academy as a content engine, and using product launches (CerebroFlow, CerebroAgent) as media moments.

**Marketing principle:** At CerebroHive, marketing is not about getting attention — it's about earning trust.

## 3. Channel Strategy

### LinkedIn (Primary Channel — All Divisions)

LinkedIn is the single highest-leverage channel for CerebroHive. The founder's personal brand and the company page must both be active.

**Content split:**
- 80% Educational / Value-first
- 20% Promotional (case studies, services, courses, results)

**Posting cadence:** 3–5x per week on the founder profile, 3x per week on the company page.

**Content categories:**

| Category | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| AI concept explanations | 2x/week | Build trust and authority |
| "Real use case" breakdowns | 1x/week | Show tangible ROI |
| AI industry commentary | 1–2x/week | Stay relevant, drive engagement |
| CerebroHive work / results | 1x/week | Convert followers to leads |
| Polls & questions | 1x/week | Drive reach via engagement signals |

**Format mix:** Text posts (highest reach), carousels (highest saves/shares), short video (fastest growing), document posts for in-depth breakdowns.

**Growth target:** 5,000 followers on founder profile by end of Year 1. 1,000+ company page followers.

---

### YouTube (Primary Channel — Academy & Awareness)

YouTube builds long-form authority and feeds the Academy funnel. Every video is a permanent asset that generates leads indefinitely.

**Content series:**

| Series | Target Audience | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| **AI Explained** | General / beginners | Top-of-funnel awareness, wide reach |
| **AI for Business** | SME founders, ops leaders | Consulting lead generation |
| **Build AI Agents** | Technical professionals, developers | Labs / product credibility |
| **Future of Work** | Corporate professionals | Academy enrollment |
| **Automation Masterclass** | Ops teams, solo founders | Automation division leads |

**Publishing cadence:** 2 videos per week minimum in Year 1. Quality over quantity — each video should be search-optimized and evergreen where possible.

**SEO targeting:** Focus on keywords with clear intent ("how to automate [business process] with AI", "AI for small business", "how to build an AI agent").

**YouTube → funnel flow:** Video CTA → free resource / lead magnet → email list → course or consultation offer.

**Growth target:** 5,000 subscribers by end of Year 1.

---

### Email Marketing

Email is the most owned, highest-conversion channel. Build and protect the list from day one.

**List building mechanisms:**
- Free lead magnets (AI readiness checklist, prompt engineering guide, AI tools directory)
- YouTube video CTAs
- LinkedIn post CTAs ("DM me for the full guide")
- Academy free mini-course or preview content
- Webinar registrations

**Newsletter:** Weekly email — "The CerebroHive Brief." Format: 1 AI insight, 1 business application, 1 tool or resource, 1 CerebroHive update/offer.

**Sequences by segment:**
- **New subscriber (generic):** 5-email welcome sequence over 10 days → qualify toward consulting, automation, or Academy track.
- **Consulting lead:** 4-email nurture sequence focused on ROI case studies and transformation stories.
- **Academy prospect:** 5-email sequence with curriculum previews, testimonials, and enrollment CTA.
- **Post-purchase (Academy):** Onboarding sequence → community → upsell to next course or certification.

**Email targets:** 2,000 subscribers by end of Year 1. 25%+ open rate. 3%+ click rate.

---

### Content Marketing / SEO (Website & Blog)

The website is the primary conversion destination. The blog builds organic search authority.

**Blog cadence:** 2–4 posts per month. Long-form (1,500–3,000 words), SEO-optimized, AI-focused.

**Content pillars:**

1. **How-to guides** — "How to Build an AI Agent for Customer Support," "How to Automate Your Finance Workflows with AI"
2. **Industry analysis** — "The State of AI Adoption in [Industry]," "Why 70% of AI Initiatives Fail"
3. **Concept explainers** — "What is an AI Agent?", "RAG vs. Fine-Tuning: What You Actually Need"
4. **Case studies** — Real client outcomes with metrics (anonymized if needed)
5. **Tool comparisons** — "Best AI Tools for SMEs in 2025," "ChatGPT vs. Claude for Business"

**SEO strategy:**
- Target long-tail, high-intent keywords (not vanity keywords)
- Build topical authority in: AI automation, AI consulting, AI training, AI agents
- Internal linking between blog posts, service pages, and Academy course pages
- Guest posting on industry publications to build backlinks (3–5 per quarter)

---

### Webinars & Online Events

Webinars serve as high-trust, high-conversion lead generation events.

**Cadence:** 1 webinar per month minimum.

**Format options:**
- **Free masterclass:** 60-minute deep dive on a single topic. CTA to Academy course or consulting call at the end.
- **Live Q&A / office hours:** 45-minute open format. Builds community, generates content clips.
- **Guest expert session:** Invite a practitioner or client. Expands reach to their audience.

**Promotion:** LinkedIn + email list + YouTube community posts. Register 5–7 days in advance.

**Conversion goal:** 5–15% of webinar attendees become leads within 30 days.

---

### Speaking & Events

Speaking engagements build authority faster than any content channel.

**Targets in Year 1:**
- 2 local business or tech events (as speaker)
- 1–2 podcasts per quarter (as guest)
- 1 AI/digital transformation conference (as speaker or panelist)

**Topic positioning:** Always speak on the intersection of business outcomes and AI — not pure technical content and not generic "AI will change everything" content. CerebroHive's angle is always: here's what AI does in practice, and here's how to make it work for your organization.

## 5. Content Production Workflow

**Weekly rhythm:**

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Monday | Write + schedule LinkedIn posts for the week |
| Tuesday | Film / record YouTube content |
| Wednesday | Write blog post or email newsletter |
| Thursday | Edit + publish YouTube video; send newsletter |
| Friday | Engage: reply to comments, DMs, LinkedIn responses |

**Content repurposing chain:** Long-form blog post → LinkedIn carousel → LinkedIn text post → email newsletter section → YouTube script outline → YouTube Shorts clip

This multiplies output from a single content investment. One good idea should appear in 5–7 formats across channels.

## 6. Paid Marketing (Year 2 Activation)

In Year 1, paid marketing is minimal. In Year 2, allocate budget to:

- **LinkedIn Ads:** Sponsored thought leadership posts targeting CTO/VP/Founder personas in SME and mid-market. Budget: $1,500–$3,000/month.
- **YouTube Ads:** Promote high-performing organic videos to cold audiences. Budget: $500–$1,000/month.
- **Retargeting:** Pixel on website to retarget visitors via LinkedIn and Google. Budget: $500/month.
- **Webinar promotion:** Paid ads for webinar registrations (LinkedIn and Google). Budget: $500–$1,000 per webinar.

Total Year 2 paid budget target: $3,000–$5,500/month. Expected blended CAC: $300–$800 depending on segment.

## 9. Marketing → Sales Handoff

Marketing qualifies leads and delivers them to sales. The handoff criteria:

**Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL):** A contact who has taken 2+ meaningful actions (e.g., attended a webinar AND downloaded a resource, or visited the consulting page 3+ times AND signed up for email).

**Sales Accepted Lead (SAL):** Sales reviews the MQL within 24 hours and confirms contact details, company fit, and that no outreach has already been attempted.

**Process:** All MQLs flow into the CRM automatically (via HubSpot or equivalent integration). Sales receives a notification. Response SLA: 24 hours for all inbound leads.
